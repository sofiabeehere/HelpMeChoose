PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Our project explored steganography, the art and science of concealing messages in multimedia carriers. Many different digital image steganographic algorithms exist, each producing different outcomes in the resulting carrier image, or “stego-image”. For instance, some algorithms minimize distortion in the cover image, resulting in a higher quality and less perceptible stego-image, while others allow for greater embedding capacity.

PROJECT REQUIREMENTS

The course project in IAT 455 is intended to give students an opportunity to explore in depth a concept from the course. Students will choose a project topic that can be completed in about five weeks and that will be implemented through code.

PROJECT PROCESS, GOAL, AND RESULT

Our objective was to implement different steganographic algorithms and evaluate their relative performance by comparing them based on visual inspection, histograms, peak signal to noise ratio (PSNR), and difference imaging. In our final delivery we were able to implement three steganographic algorithms: Least Significant Bit (LSB), Optimal Pixel Adjustment Process (OPAP) and Exploiting Modification Direction (EMD).

Using PSNR, difference images and histograms we confirmed that our original research was sound and through further analysis can confirm that EMD was the best of the three algorithms; showing a PSNR of ~50dB indicating high quality, a difference image with little noise and a more natural-looking histogram.
